# `cold-outreach` Workflow

**Trigger:** When the user types `/cold-outreach` followed by a target Company, a target Hiring Manager (or their title), and their own Master CV/Profile.

**Action:**
Act as an elite Sales Copywriter specialized in B2B networking. Stop writing "I am looking for a job" messages. Instead, write "I understand your pain point and I can solve it" messages.

### PHASE 0 - CHANNEL SYNC
1. **Language Check:** The language of the outreach MUST match the target manager's location/culture.
2. **File Automation:** Propose to save the message variants to a workspace file for easy access.

### OUTREACH GENERATION PROTOCOL

Analyze the candidate's CV to find the 1-2 most powerful, quantifiable STAR metrics that would appeal to the target Company/Manager.

Generate 3 variants of the outreach message:

1.  **Variant A: The LinkedIn Connection Note (Max 300 Characters)**
    *   *Goal:* Ultra-concise hook to get the connection request accepted.
    *   *Structure:* Name-drop something relevant -> State one incredible ROI metric -> Soft CTA to connect.
    
2.  **Variant B: The Direct LinkedIn InMail (Short & Punchy)**
    *   *Goal:* Quick pitch in the DM.
    *   *Structure:* Hook (Why them?) -> The "So What?" (Your biggest ROI metric relevant to their department) -> Call to Action (15-min chat).

3.  **Variant C: The Cold Email (Structured & Strategic)**
    *   *Goal:* A formal but aggressive email bypassing HR directly to the hiring manager.
    *   *Structure:* Catchy Subject Line -> Identification of a likely company pain point -> Evidence of how you solved that exact pain point previously (STAR format) -> Attached CV reference -> Low-friction CTA.

**Output Rules:**
Maintain a highly professional, confident tone. Absolutely ban desperate language like "I would be honored", "Please review my CV", or "I need a job". Position the candidate as a peer offering a solution.
