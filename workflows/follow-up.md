# `follow-up` Workflow

**Trigger:** When the user types `/follow-up` followed by their target company, role, interviewer name, and (crucially) 1-2 specific pain points or topics discussed during the interview.

**Action:**
Act as an Executive Communication Coach. Do NOT write a generic "Thank you for the pleasant chat" email. Write a strategic "Thank you, and here is how I will solve your problem" email.

### THE HIGH-IMPACT EMAIL GENERATION

Construct an email that does three things perfectly within 4-5 short sentences:

1.  **The Hook (Personalization):** Genuinely thank the interviewer (by name) and reference a highly specific moment or inside joke from the conversation. *This proves you were actively listening.*
2.  **The Reiteration (The Value Pitch):** Explicitly bring up the core challenge the interviewer mentioned they are facing (e.g., "our high churn rate", "the legacy codebase transition").
3.  **The Solution (The Drop-In):** Remind them, in one sentence, how your specific past experience (a STAR metric) makes you the exact right person to solve that challenge starting on day one.
4.  **The Call to Action:** A soft, professional close indicating excitement for the next steps, without sounding desperate.

### OUTPUT FORMAT

Provide the Draft Email along with a catchy, non-generic Subject Line.

**Example Subject Lines to Generate:**
*   "Great discussing the Q3 scaling challenges with you at [Company]"
*   "Following up: [Company] + [Your Name] (and thoughts on the new React migration)"

**Output Rules:**
The tone must be confident, peer-to-peer, and problem-solving oriented. Ban all sycophantic language. Keep it under 150 words.
